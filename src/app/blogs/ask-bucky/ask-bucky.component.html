Ask Bucky Prompts for Global Solution Implementation:

NEWWORLD GAME — Possible/Suggested PROMPTS

STEP 1: Defining the Problem State

Question 1:

What is the problem you have chosen and why is it important?
After you have answered the above question from your personal perspective, knowledge, experience and values, ask Bucky:

PROMPTS

  • Why is (insert chosen problem) important? And/or: In addition to what I have listed: (copy your answer to the question into the Ask Bucky question box), why else is the (insert chosen problem) important?
  • What are all the ways it (insert chosen problem) is important to the world (and a specific location, if appropriate)? Include economic, environment, human rights, health, security/peace and other impacts.

Question 2:

What are the symptoms of this problem? How do you measure it?

PROMPTS

  • What are the symptoms of the (insert chosen problem)?
  • What are the causes of these symptoms? List the economic, political, environmental, educational, health and other possible causes of the problem.
  • How do we measure the impacts of (insert chosen problem)? Or: How do we measure the impacts of the symptoms (insert symptoms of the problem).

Question 3:

How many people does this problem impact in the world? Where is it most severe?

PROMPTS

  • How many people does this problem impact in the world?
  • Where are these people
  • Where is it most severe?
  • Why are these people impacted?

Question 4:

What will happen if nothing is done to deal with this problem?

PROMPTS

  • What will happen if nothing is done to deal with this problem? (insert what the problem is from the answers to your previous prompts)
  • What will happen to the people, the environment, the economy, and any other factors you can identify?

Question 5:

What are the causes of this problem?

  • What are the causes of this problem? Why do we have this problem? What are the technological, social, political, environmental drivers or aspects of the problem?

STEP 2: Envisioning the Preferred State

Question 1:

What is the preferred or ideal state that you want to reach with your solution? What is your goal? What will the world look like if this problem is solved?
After you have answered the above question from your personal perspective, knowledge, experience and values, ask Bucky:

PROMPTS

  • What is the preferred or ideal state that you want to reach with your solution to the problem (insert chosen problem)?
  • What does the world look like if the problem (insert chosen problem) is resolved?
  • What is the goal of a solution to the problem (insert chosen problem)? What does it achieve? What are the positive results of a successful solution to the (insert chosen problem)? What are those impacts on the general well-being of people, the environment, economy?
  • What does it do to fix or resolve the (insert chosen problem)?
  • What are the results of a successful solution to the (insert chosen problem)? What does a solution look like?

Question 2:

How will you measure success? How will you know when you reach the preferred state?

PROMPTS

  • How will you measure the success of a solution to (insert chosen problem) that gets us to the preferred or ideal state? (insert a summary of the preferred state)
  • How will we know when we have reached the preferred state of the (insert chosen problem)?

STEP 3: Developing Our Solution

Question 1:

What does our solution do to reach the preferred state? How will it do it?

PROMPTS

  • What is needed to reach the preferred or ideal state (insert here your preferred state)?
  • What do we need to do to resolve the (insert chosen problem) and reach the (Insert the preferred state)?
  • What actions are needed to resolve the (insert chosen problem) and achieve the (Insert the preferred state)?
  • What are the results of a successful solution? What does a solution look like? What does it do?

Question 2:

What technology, programs, policies will it need?

PROMPTS

  • What existing technology, programs, policies will our solution need to achieve the (Insert the preferred state)?
  • What new technology, programs, policies will our solution need?
  • What existing technology, programs, policies can be modified so that they achieve the (Insert the preferred state)?

Question 3:

What resources does our solution need?

PROMPTS

  • What resources will our solution (insert description of the solution) need? What kind of material, technology, environmental, human, and other kinds of resources will our solution need?
  • Are these resources available?

Question 4:

How is our solution part of a circular, regenerative, more equitable economy?

PROMPTS

  • How is our solution part of a circular, regenerative, more equitable economy?
  • How does the solution (insert solution description here) reduce or eliminate waste, regenerate local and global ecosystems and economy, and achieve a more equitable world?

STEP 4: Implementation

Question 1:

How much will our strategy cost to test, for a proof-of-concept, in the country where we will test and first implement the solution?

PROMPTS

  • How much will our strategy (insert summary of solution/strategy) cost to test, for a proof-of-concept, in the country where we will test and first implement the solution?
  • What would be a least-cost test of our solution (insert summary of solution/strategy)?

Question 2:

How much will our strategy cost to implement at scale

PROMPTS

  • How much will our strategy cost to implement throughout the country where we are testing it, so that it achieves the preferred state?
  • How much will our strategy (insert summary of solution/strategy) cost to implement globally—so that our preferred state (insert preferred state) is achieved everywhere the problem (insert short summary of problem) exists?

Question 3:

Where will we get the resources and funding needed to implement our solution, to do the above? (Funding Prompts)

PROMPTS

  • Where will we get the resources and funding needed to implement our solution (insert summary of solution/strategy), to do the above?
  • Where can we obtain funding to test our solution?
  • What foundations, government agencies, or other funding sources would fund (insert a summary of your solutions here) in (insert the country where you will test and first implement the solution here)? What is their contact information?

Question 4:

Who will implement our solution? Where will it be tested (and first implemented)? Who will be our in-country/on-the-ground partner? (Partner Prompts)

PROMPTS

  • What non-governmental organizations, national, regional, and local government agencies or groups in (insert the country where you will test and first implement the solution here) would be a good partner for a program that (insert a summary of your solutions here)? What is their contact information?
  • Or: Who will implement our solution (insert summary of solution/strategy)? Where will it be tested (and first implemented)? Who could be our in-country/on-the-ground partner? Make a list of potential collaborators and/or partners in (insert country where you want to test your strategy/solution).

Question 5:

What actions are needed in the next 6-12 months to get our solution implemented? Who will do what, when, where?

PROMPTS

  • What actions are needed in the next 6-12 months to get our solution implemented? Who will do what, when, where?

Question 6:

What does our implemented strategy look like, in more detail? (For this task ask one of our Ai colleagues to draw a picture of what the strategy will look like when implemented) Vision of Solution Prompts

PROMPTS

  • An example of a prompt that produced the results found in the NWG Operating Manual in the document “Custom GPT for Design Science Storyboard” is the following: “Hi - please make a story about a village in Africa that wants solar panels on their health clinic.” This was done at Go here.
  • What does our implemented strategy (insert summary of solution/strategy) look like, in more detail? (For this task ask one of our Ai colleagues to draw a picture of what the strategy will look like when implemented)
  • For your solution, adapt the above prompt to reflect your solution’s implementation in the country, region, or community location you have chosen. For images and more details on what the above prompt and its variations can generate go here.

Question 7:

Results 1. What are the results of implementing our strategy? What would be the results of providing everyone in a community ____________ (insert description of your strategy) on the local economy, jobs, environment, human health, and other social factors?

PROMPTS

  • Results 1. What are the results of implementing our strategy? What would be the results of providing everyone in a community ____________ (insert description of your strategy) on the local economy, jobs, environment, human health, and other social factors?

Question 8:

Results 2. What would be the results of providing everyone in the world ____________ (insert description of your strategy) on the global economy, additional jobs, environment, human health, and other social factors?

PROMPTS

  • Results 2. What would be the results of providing everyone in the world ____________ (insert description of your strategy) on the global economy, additional jobs, environment, human health, and other social factors?

Question 9:

What would we do with $5000 to advance the strategy towards implementation?

PROMPTS

  • What would we do with $5000 to advance the strategy towards implementation?
  • What should we spend $5,000 on to advance our strategy so that it moves a step closer to full implementation?

Question 10:

What can you/your team do — starting now, with just the resources to which you have access, to move your strategy forward?

PROMPTS

  • What can you/your team do — starting now, with just the resources to which you have access, to move your strategy forward?
