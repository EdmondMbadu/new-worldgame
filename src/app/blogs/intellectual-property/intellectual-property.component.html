NewWorld Game Honor Code

Share freely. Credit honestly. Protect when you must.

NewWorld Game is built for open problem-solving, but the work your team creates still belongs to your team. This page explains the honor code, attribution norms, and intellectual property options for participants.

Why this matters

The lab works because ideas move, and trust keeps them moving.

Solutions built here are contributions to a shared global learning environment. Teams are encouraged to learn from one another, cite useful work, and improve promising approaches so a solution can travel beyond one classroom or event.

At the same time, teams may want to carry a solution forward as a venture, patent, research project, or public-interest product. That is compatible with the World Game tradition. Protect the parts that need protection, and share what you can with clear credit.

The honor code

Four promises participants make to each other

I

Do your own team's work

Do not copy another team's solution, in whole or in part, and present it as your own.

II

Credit generously

Build on another team, library entry, dataset, design, or code, and say so clearly. Borrowing with attribution is collaboration.

III

Respect protected work

If work is marked protected, confidential, or embargoed, do not share, reuse, or disclose it outside the Lab without permission.

IV

Compete with goodwill

The goal is better solutions for the world. Treat other participants, their work, and their ambitions with respect.

Honor code violations may result in tournament disqualification, removal from the Lab, or removal of work from the Solution Library.

Intellectual property

The questions teams usually ask

01

Who owns my team's work?

+

Your team does. NewWorld Game does not claim ownership of participant solutions. Copyright in your writing, designs, code, and media belongs to the creators automatically. Publishing to the Library grants NewWorld Game permission to display and share the work as part of the Library.

02

Can other teams use our ideas?

+

Ideas themselves are not owned in the same way a document, codebase, design, or recording can be. Cross-pollination is expected and encouraged. The rule is simple: build on each other and give visible credit.

03

What if we want to commercialize?

+

You can. Consider publishing the problem, impact case, and public summary while keeping implementation details in a protected annex. If patents, trademarks, or university ownership rules may apply, speak with a qualified advisor before public disclosure.

04

What is NewWorld Game's role?

+

NewWorld Game provides the platform, process, and records of submitted work. We can enforce Lab rules and honor code expectations, but we do not provide legal advice or adjudicate legal disputes between teams or third parties.

Embargo option

Protect it first. Then share it when ready.

Teams may request an embargo when they submit. The full solution can be withheld from public Library view for up to 12 months after the Lab concludes, giving the team time to file, partner, pilot, or launch.

When the window ends, the solution is expected to join the open Library unless a different written arrangement applies. The aim is practical: protect what needs time, then let useful knowledge serve more people.
