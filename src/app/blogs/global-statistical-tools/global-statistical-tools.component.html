Global Statistical Data and Data Analysis Tools

Accurate statistics on the state of the world are indispensable for dealing with the problems of the world. They are the foundation of NewWorld Game and the development of solutions and strategies that positively impact the world.

Accurate, verifiable, up-to-date statistics from reliable, trustworthy sources serve as the foundation upon which governments, international organizations, corporations, communities, policymakers, researchers, and activists base their decisions, strategies, and interventions. Without good global statistics, solutions to problems such as poverty, hunger, climate change, health care, and education will be misguided and inefficient at best— while solutions that contain misinformation, conspiracy theory, whimsey, or wishful thinking are not just counter-productive, they perpetuate the divisions of a fractured world and the problems we face.

Reliable statistics enable the identification of problem areas and the extent, depth, and impacts of the problem, tracking of progress over time, and assessment of the effectiveness of solutions, policies and programs. They also foster transparency, accountability, and public trust, by providing a clear picture of where the world stands in its pursuit of sustainable development goals and solving the world’s problems. In essence, accurate statistics are not just numbers—they are a crucial tool for informed decision-making, strategic planning, and ultimately, making a tangible difference in solving the world's most pressing problems.

Below is a listing of the most reliable global statistical sources and links to access them. These statistics can be useful to NewWorld Game “players”— designers, researchers, high school and college students, policy makers, community leaders, and everyone in between— who don’t readily find answers to some of the questions they feel need answering for solutions that are as strong and compelling as they want.

The listing makes no claim to being definitive. It is just our 10+ years-in-the-making collection. Your feedback and additions are most welcome.

    • Part 2: Data analysis tools

  • Facets: Know Your Data. "Better data leads to better models" is the motto of Facets, a toolkit for "understanding and analyzing machine learning datasets." Facets offers two powerful data visualization tools. The first, Overview, gives users a quick visual analysis of the distribution of values across the features of one or more datasets. Overview provides users with summary statistics that give the general shape of each feature of their dataset and may help identify issues like unexpected values, missing values for a large number of observations, training/serving skew, and train/test/validation skew. The second tool, Dive, is an interactive interface for exploring large numbers of data points at once. Dive visualizes the relationships between data points across all different features of a dataset and allows a data point to be bucketed in multiple dimensions. The tool can help users identify classifier failure, systematic errors, and potential new signals for ranking. Facets is an open-source software toolkit developed by the People + AI Research (PAIR) team at Google Research. [HCL]
