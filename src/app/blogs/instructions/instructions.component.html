How To Use NewWorld Game

This is a cheatsheet of everything in the NewWorld Game web application. If there is something that you wish to do but is not included here, please follow this link and add your suggestion.

1. Create an Account

To Create an account go to: Join Now and enter your first name, last name, email, and password (twice). An email will then be sent to you to verify your account.

2. Sign In

Once you have verified your email, you can now Sign In to your account (Check your spam in case you do not see the verification email).

To Sign In go to: Sign In and enter your email, and password. Voila, that is all you need to start developing solutions.

3. Home Page

Once you have signed in to your account, you will see the Home Page. The home page is composed of three sections - left, middle, and right.
- The left section has basic information about you (name, email, etc.), the ability to join a tournament, and pending solutions ( these are solutions that you are working on but have not submitted).
- The middle seciton contains all solutions submitted by everyone, including yours ( if you have submitted any solution).
- The right section contains a button to start a solution, and right below that are solution(s) that needs to be evaluated by you.

4. Start a Solution

To start a solution, go the right of your home screen, and click on "Start a Solution Lab". This will lead you to a page with 6 fields to fill.
1. The first field is a working title for your solution.
2. The second field is a description of your solution. Use Chat with Bucky if you need help describing your solution in few bullet points. For help on how to ask question to Chat with Bucky see here.
3. The third field is "Add Team Members". You can add emails of all the members you want to invite. The members will then receive an email asking them to join your solution. If an invited member does not have an account, they should create one to join you.
4. This is not a field per say, but are randomly generated evaluators that will be evaluating your solution once it is submiited
5. The fifth field is for Sustaimable Development Goals (SDGs) that correspond to your solution.
6. The last field is when you plan to finish and submit the solution.
Once all fields completed, click "Launch Solution Lab" to start developping your soluion.

5. Develop a Solution

Once you launch the solution, you are now ready to develop a solution. There are 5 steps - 4 development steps and one final review step. While developing your solution at each step, you can consult Chat With Bucky as a resource, or hover and click on the question mark next to each question to access 3 extra sources: ( Data Commons, Atlas SDG, SDG Tracker )

5.1 Define the Problem State

This is where you define the problem you are trying to solve. Each question allows you to dig deeper into the nature of the problem. To save your progress, scroll down and you will see the save button.
To go to the next step (step 2), scroll down and click next.

5.2 Envision the Preferred State

Now that you are done with, or have decided to move on from the first step, you are now on step 2: "Envision the Preferred State". This is where you envision where you want to get once your solution has been achieved/done. To track which step you are on, you can see where the red bar timeline points to. You can also move back and forth of each step by clicking at the appropriate step on the timeline.

5.3 Develop Our Plan

This is the third step on the list. This is where you develop a plan that will get you from the Problem State to the Preferred State. It contains 5 questions.

5.4 Implementation

This step is all about implementation. The resources- financial, materials, humans, needed to implement the plan.

5.5 Strategy Review

This is the final step where all the work comes together into a coherent whole

6. Save Solution

At each step, you can save your progess by simply scrolling to the bottom of the page and clicking save. The saved work will be under Pending Solutions (highlited in red below) on the Home Page. You can then exit this page knowing that your progress has been saved.

7. Submit Solution

To Submit a solution, scroll to the bottom of the page ( Strategy Review Page) and submit your solution.

8. View Solution

To view your submitted solution, you can look in two places: Home Page, or your Profile Page. On the Home Page, you can scroll down and inspect until you find your solution, or you can simply look at your solution in the search bar by typing the title of the solution. You can also, just sort the solution by clicking at the Sort By button at the very top (middle).

Below is the Profile Page. To get there, click under your name at the top right, and then click on Profile. This will lead to your profile page where you can add different credentials, change your profile picture, and also where all solutions that have been developed by you or where you were a member are located.

9. Edit Submitted Solution

To Edit a Submitted Solution, first you need to find that solution (See section 8 for that). Once you have located your solution, click on more to see the full view of the solution. Once on the full view of the solution, you will see an Edit Button right below the title. click on Edit to edit your solution. You will then find that solution under Pending Solutions on the Home Page.

10. Delete Submitted Solution

There are cases where you want to delete your solution completely. The steps are similar to editing a solution. The only difference is that at the final stage, instead of Editing, click on Delete Solution.

11. Delete Pending Solution

You can also delete a pending solution. To do so, go to the pending section ( see below), and click on the trash icon of the given solution to delete the pending solution.

12. Evaluate Submitted Solution

If you are somehow selected to evaluate a solution ( you will receive an email stating that you have been invited to evaluate a solution), you can find the solution at the right section on your Home Page under "Strategy Evaluation" ( see highlited section in red below)

Once you click on the parcticular solution to evaluate you will be led to a screen where you can evaluate the solution. See here on how to evaluate a solution properly.
Once done, the evaluation will be appended to the solution as part of a grade that will be used for further consideration such as the tournament. So your grade is a very critical part of the proceess

13. Join a Tournament

To join a tournament, you need to have at least two evaluations from a given solution and have an average of 9 from these evaluations. Assuming that these requirements are met, click on Join a Tournanment on the left of your Home page ( see picture below) to Join a tournament.

Once on that page, simply scroll to the bottom under "Your Solutions" and select the solution you want to send to the tournament and click "Add Solution To Tournament". You will be contacted if you win the tournament or if something is needed from you. Solutions that have been sent already to the tournament will then show up at the top under "Tournaments Joined"

14. Add Image To Text Editor

To add an image to the text editor, you can drag and drop the desired image. However, this method is not advised since the dropped image has to be very small ( less than 100KB). To add an image of any size ( although do not abuse), go to this link and upload your image by clicking on Choose Images. Links will be generated; make sure to copy Direct link. Then go to the text editor, and paste the link. You will then be able to resize the image at your choosing using your mouse. You can also use other providers as you choose ( e.g. dropbox, google drive), but the one suggested (postimage) is quick and requires no setup. *Note that the images shared are public and should not contain sensible or private information.
