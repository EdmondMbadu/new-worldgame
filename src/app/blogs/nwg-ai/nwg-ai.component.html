NewWorld Game AI

The NewWorld Game uses AI (or more precisely Large Language Models— LLMs) to assist in answering many of the questions needed for solving global and local problems. These general purpose and powerful tools can assist in nearly all phases of the NewWorld Game creative process. The key ingredient, at this point in LLMs development, is to ask the right questions, to pose the best prompts that will shed light on what you are looking for. Here are some general guides:

1. Best Practices to Use Ask Bucky, ChatGPT, Gemini, and other LLM tools1

  1. Be clear and specific: Clearly communicate your request or questions
  2. Start with Instructions: Tell the model how you want it to behave.
  3. Provide Context: Give the model relevant information about the topic.
  4. Use Complete Sentences. Frame your prompts to ensure the model understands.
  5. Use Previous Answers: Reference the model’s previous responses.
  6. Experiment and Iterate: Experiment with different phrasing or variations.
  7. Use Explicit Instructions: Clearly state your instructions in the prompt.
  8. Limit The Response length: Ask the model to provide specific key points.
  9. Refine and Review: Adjust your prompts for more accurate answers.
  10. Verify and Fast-check: Make sure the information is correct.

2. GENERAL PROMPTS to consider using:

  • Explain [COMPLEX TOPIC] like I’m a 5-year-old.
  • Support your analysis with relevant data and statistics.
  • Provide a comprehensive list of key players in the industry.
  • What are some breakthroughs in the (technology your design is or could use) in the last two (or four) years?

3.1. Defining the Problem: Prompt Example

  • How many people are “hungry” (insert your chosen problem area) in the world? What are the sources of the answer.

    • If there are multiple answers choose the one(s) that have the most recent source publication dates and are the most relevant to your interests. If you don’t get that information you need, ask different questions, such as the following:

  • How many people are (for hunger-related problems) malnourished, starving, stunted, in danger of food shortages, etc. (plus sources for each question asked). [Insert alternative descriptors for your chosen problem area.]
  • Give me an overview of the world food (or other) problem based on latest UN reports.
  • What is the most serious problem related to world hunger (insert your chosen problem area)?
  • Consider more complicated prompts— like: How many people are hungry in the world? What are the sources of the answer. Write for a well-informed audience. Use an academic tone. Make it concise. Summarize at end. 1
  • Remember that ChatGPT and Gemini have a memory. They keeps track of what you wrote. You can use/build on this memory in your revisions to your prompts.

3.2. Present State

  • What are the major parts of the global _________ (insert your chosen problem area)?
  • What influences the state of the global ______ system?
  • What is the gap between the problem state and preferred state? For this prompt you will need to insert the present state from the “Defining the problem” prompt and your vision of the preferred state

3.3. Strategy

AI works well as a generator of ideas and options. It is good at volume. Not necessarily good ideas but lots of them—and buried within the many might be a good to very good idea, or one that will trigger a new idea in your mind, or some ideas that working together will be great combinations. The following are some prompts you with which you can start.

  • What are the alternatives for __________ (examples from food problem area: eliminating hunger in the world; increasing food production; increasing the amount of land being irrigated; reducing post-harvest food waste; making food affordable for everyone, etc. (Tip #1: Take one of the facets of your preferred state and insert it into the prompt. Tip #2: Tell the AI “You are an expert at strategic thinking, problem solving, design, and option generation. When asked to solve a problem you come up with creative, clever, interesting ideas. Your first task is to ________ (e.g eliminate hunger from the world). Describe the details of each way.
  • What is needed to increase food production in ____ (location where problem area is severest).
  • Who is needed to do this (the above)?
  • What resources are needed to make this happen? (to implement Increased food production)
  • What will it cost to do _________ How much money would it cost to reach the ideal state/eliminate hunger, feed everyone in the world?
  • Where could the money needed to do _______ come from?
  • What are possible outcomes of _______ (e.g. eliminating hunger, implementing x strategy or solution).

3.6. Additional possible prompts to explore2

  • Talk to an Expert

    • “Hi ChatGPT, I want you to answer like a [Expert’s NAME (Jeffrey Sachs, Buckminster Fuller, etc.)]. Use all the knowledge of [NAME] and any and every available information about how [NAME] thinks and solves problems. My challenge is: [YOUR CHALLENGE]. Now, please provide me a detailed 500-word answer with a plan of action.”

  • Create a successful GSL solution/strategy/product launch strategy

    • Prompt: “[INSERT A BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF YOUR GSL SOLUTION/STRATEGY/PRODUCT AND TARGET AUDIENCE] Guide me through developing a solution/product launch strategy using the Product Launch Formula to generate interest and implementation.”

  • Business Idea Generator

    • Prompt: “I want you to act as a world-class business strategist specializing in innovation and entrepreneurship. My first request is for you to generate a business idea. Here’s some context: Industry — [Industry] Interest — [Interests] “Include Problem Statement, Solution, Unique Selling Point (USP), Target Market, and Revenue Model. Include some context and a list of [Number] possible business ideas.” I need you to write content with a good balance of “perplexity” and “burstiness.”

  • Create a full Project Plan

    • Prompt: “Create a project plan for [TOPIC]. Include tasks, deliverables, and milestones. Also, include how long it would likely take to reach each milestone.”

  • Ultimate Business Development Manager

    • Prompt: “You are an expert in innovation and developing new solutions to global problems. You have helped many people and organization before me to identify solutions and develop strategies to reach them. Your task is now to identify an innovative strategy for [INSERT GLOBAL PROBLEM—E.G. HUNGER, LACK OF CLEAN WATER, ETC]. To better understand what I want and need, you should always answer by including a question that helps you better understand the context and my needs. Did you understand?”

  • Business Strategist

    • Prompt: “[INSERT A BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF YOUR SOLUTION AND TARGET AUDIENCE] I want you to act as a business [OR GOVERNMENT] strategist expert in getting innovative solutions implemented. My first request is to develop a comprehensive plan to get [INSERT YOUR SOLUTION] implemented in [INSERT LOCATION—COUNTRY, GEOGRAPHICAL REGION, ETC.].

3.7. Warnings

  • Sources for answers requiring the most up-to-date statistical data might not be available. It will depend on when the model was last updated. If this is the case, settle for what is available and then go to the latest statistical document. A good starting place is to check the Primer’s Appendix #2, List of Online Statistical Data Sources. These will take you to the latest available data.
  • The current state of LLMs is evolving rapidly. Whatever is written here needs to be viewed in this context— it could be/most likely will be at least a bit out-of-date by the time you are reading this. Nevertheless, this introductory overview should be helpful as a place to start.

Remember that each question/prompt for the LLM is an exploration. If you don’t get an answer that is helpful, ask the prompt in a different way. Invest some time in “prompt engineering”— editing and playing with prompts to come up with the results you want.
