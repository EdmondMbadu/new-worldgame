NewWorld Game Evaluator

"We are called to be the architects of the future, not its victims." - Buckminster Fuller

"We are also called to be evaluators, discerners of future options." - Medard Gabel

A critical part of NewWorld Game is the Evaluator. As an Evaluator you become part of the Design Team that develops the solution. Your evaluation and comments are essential for improving the solution so it can be implemented in the real world.

On a more personal level, your role as an evaluator enables you to learn and develop a critical skill for living in a world of great complexity— of innumerable options plus misinformation, inaccuracies, misleading claims and conclusions, and inadequate evidence. By evaluating other team’s solutions, you develop and practice responsible discernment— an essential skill for making important decisions in our complicated world. You, and your diversity of perspectives, knowledge and skills, are also vital to the development of viable solutions.

The following is a guide for what you are to do as an Evaluator in NewWorld Game:
Your role as an Evaluator is to look over the strategy that has been submitted and, as best you can, come up with ways for improving it, making it stronger, and more likely to get implemented in the real world. There are two forms of evaluation that each solution, including yours, goes through.

Numerical Evaluations— Sliders

NewWorld Game has a set of measures that are used to evaluate a solution. These include the following:

  • Does the solution achieve the Preferred State
  • Is it technologically feasible — does the solution use current technology (no magic wands)
  • Is it ecologically positive — does it do no harm, preserve, or better, regenerate the environment (destroying the environment is no solution)
  • Economical — is it affordable, is there a reasonable source for funding the strategy (no Santa Claus)
  • Equitable — is it fair, just, builds social cohesion, reduces wealth disparities (no subsidies to billionaires)
  • Understandable — is it straight-forward, organized so it can be understood by a high school student (and if you are a HS student—by your parents)

Each of the above has a sliding scale of 1 to 10. Move the slider to the number you think is an accurate representation of where the solution is relative to the evaluation criteria. (1 = no, not viable; 5 = could be, but needs work: 10 = yes, let’s do it, now)

This quantitative scoring system is very important. But more important is your qualitative feedback and suggestions for improvements to the solution. You add these in the “Comment” windows. If you put in a low score for a solution on any criteria there is a good reason for it. Put that/those reason(s) in the Comments. The designers who developed the solution will use your comments to improve their solution—and re-submit it to be evaluated again. When it reaches a high enough numerical level, it is eligible to be submitted to the NewWorld Tournament.

NewWorld Game Solution Evaluation Guide

The following is an outline of general considerations when evaluating a solution. Not everything will be relevant to every solution. The Guide was put together to provide an idea to Evaluators as to what the gradations in the 1-10 scoring system corresponds.

Score Bracket Description
1-3 Initial Conceptualization — Basic problem definition and rudimentary Preferred State. Limited understanding of the problem and/or potential impact. Identification of symptoms, not underlying causes. Solution with minimal development and possible use of options not currently technologically possible or with severe environmental impacts. Significant problems with costs and social equity/fairness. Gaps in understanding, feasibility, partners and funding. Lack of detailed planning or proposed test. Implementation strategy not clear. Few if any partners/stakeholders/funders identified.
4-6 Preliminary Design — More developed problem and Preferred State definitions. Basic solution idea and conceptual framework with practical elements. Basic understanding of the problem and potential solutions. Preliminary implementation strategy but with gaps. Probably technologically feasible, possible unknown impacts on environment and sequity. © 2024 EarthGame Some data and evidence support the potential impact, but it remains limited. Initial identification of potential partners and funders for stakeholder collaboration.
7-8 Advanced Development — Complete problem and Preferred State descriptions. Well- developed solution with detailed planning, well-defined test/proof-of-concept, cost estimates, and timeline. Strong understanding of the problem and most feasible options. Evidence and data demonstrate a clear potential for impact and effectiveness. Solution, if implemented at scale, has good chance of achieving the Preferred State and one or more of the SDGs. Significant partner identification. Identified challenges with effective strategies to address them. Solution is scalable and adaptable.
9-10 Ready to Implement — Problem state, Preferred State complete. Solution is fully developed, uses available technology, is implementable at scale, with costs of test and implementation worked out. Solid, even compelling, evidence of effectiveness, identification of specific technology, preferred local partners for test and first implementation at scale, and funders. Challenges have been addressed. Solutions are innovative, technologically feasible, flexible, respectful of partners lead to increased human well-being and should be implemented as soon as possible.
