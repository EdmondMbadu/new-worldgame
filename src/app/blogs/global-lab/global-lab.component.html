The 2025 Global Solutions Lab

The 2025 Global Solutions Lab will use the research, problem-solving/strategic planning, and implementation platform— the NewWorld Game —which combines the Lab’s methodology with our unique custom-trained AIs, plus over 100 global statistical databases, a set of 400+ state-of-the-world reports from every UN agency, and many international research organizations, as well as a dozen+ libraries of global solutions.

With your perspective, knowledge, values, and experience, the process of researching and developing new, innovative, affordable, regenerative, and immediately implementable strategies for achieving one or more of the UN SDGs will be faster, more thorough, and more creative. AND— the NewWorld Tournament, where the best strategy will be awarded a cash prize, adds yet another exciting level to the Lab.

NewWorld Tournament

Your Lab solution can be entered in the NewWorld Tournament where it will be eligible for the award of a cash prize used to advance your strategy towards implementation in the real world.

More Details

The Global Solutions Lab is generally focused on making real the quote by Buckminster Fuller and specifically on achieving the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals. It is an intense experience— participants will be expected to make a substantial time contribution every day throughout the Lab.

Format: The Lab will be in-person and online. It will use the NewWorld Game global research and problem-solving platform for creative work. Participants collaborate in an international, cross-cultural, multi-disciplinary, and intergenerational Research and Design Team. The Lab culminates with presentations of the work to a group of UN, corporate, philanthropic, academic, and NGO leaders.

Tuition: $250. Partial tuition scholarships are available.*
*It is our conviction that no one should be denied access to the Lab for financial reasons. Scholarship funds are limited and variable according to expenses and donations. You can contribute to the Global Solutions Lab Scholarship Fund by donating here.

The Global Solutions Lab was developed by and is conducted by EarthGame, a non-profit research and education NGO officially associated with the United Nations Department of Global Communications.

On-line Lab Participation

The Lab has participants from all over the world. Different parts of the world don’t all have the same level of telecommunications infrastructure, so here is what you will need to participate:

  • Reliable internet connection (as high speed as possible)
  • A computer or tablet (with video & audio) and a relatively quiet space
  • A time commitment of at least 4 to 6 hours per day, starting around 10:00 am Eastern U.S. time

The powerful quote from Buckminster Fuller encapsulates the ethos of the Global Solutions Lab: as we stand on the brink of technological revolutions, it is imperative to approach AI and other advances as architects, actively shaping our future rather than passively enduring it.

2024 NewWorld Tournament Winner

Electrifying Health Clinics in Democratic Republic of Congo
The first NewWorld Tournament award goes to a team in the D.R. Congo who are using the award to electrify a village health clinic (where 60% of the health clinics don’t have electricity). It will serve as a proof-of-concept to scale their solution to meet health clinic electricity needs for the rest of the country. Learn more here.

What It Is: More About the Lab

Students and young professionals from around the world will be coming to the 2025 Lab. They will be briefed by UN experts (from WHO, UNDP, UNEP, UNESCO, and other agencies), research and develop comprehensive strategies for dealing with critical global problems, and propose paths for reaching the UN’s SDGs — then present their work to peer review and a committee of experts.

When & Where: June 17–21, 2025, in-person and online.
