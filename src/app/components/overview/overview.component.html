Welcome to the NewWorld Game.

The NewWorld Game is a platform, a work- and play-space, for discovery and developing solutions to real-world problems— and getting those solutions implemented. Its goal is to have real impact—to change the world— by providing you and millions of other like-minded creative individuals with space and tools for developing solutions to the problem and issues about which you care. It is a place that focuses your creativity, experience, values, and perspective on problems and issues that matter— and provides you a set of tools, and how to use them, that lead to new paths of action and impact— to strategic designs, plans, and solutions that are viable, regenerative, inclusive, resilient, and cost-effective— and that get implemented. That it is also fun, helps.

The NewWorld Game’s name draws on its history as being a direct descendent and outgrowth of the original World Game that was inspired by and based on the work and vision of Buckminster Fuller. It’s developers worked with Fuller on the first iterations of the World Game. It points to the need for a new world, one that not only replaces the old world, but makes new options and solutions that engender new possibilities for everyone. The NewWorld Game does this by empowering you with ways of developing new options for a better future that works for all life on Earth, not just those with large quantities of money and the delusion of thinking they are in control.

Turn Your Ideas into Real Solutions

1. Parts and Wholes

The NewWorld Game (NWG) takes technological advancements of the 21 st century and creatively couples them with the design science engine that was used to play the original World Game “longhand” in the month-long World Game workshops and shorter in-person and online Global Solutions Labs. It couples an easy-to-use interface with AI, an empowered methodology for developing real-world solutions, comprehensive world statistical resources, solution libraries, and a community of problem solvers. It builds and leverages the power of a diverse, collaborative, cross-cultural, multi-disciplinary, and intergenerational global community with linkages to real organizations implementing solutions.

2. Your Role

Your role in the NewWorld Game is to develop solutions to global and local problems. Ideally, you do this as part of a team, but it is possible to play the NewWorld Game alone. Your solution will be Improved/Evaluated by groups of your peers. When your solution is ready, it can be added to the NewWorld Solutions Library and entered in the NewWorld Game Tournament. The fact that you are here is important. On several levels— for the world, for you, and for the community you will be a part of as you work together on what you care about. Thank you.

3. AI

The NewWorld Game uses AI (or more precisely Large Language Models— LLMs) to assist in answering many of the questions needed for solving global and local problems. These general purpose and powerful tools can assist in nearly all phases of the NewWorld Game creative process. The key ingredient, at this point in LLMs development, is to ask the right questions, to pose the best prompts that will shed light on what you are looking for. Each stage of the NewWorld Game process needs different prompts to elicit useful results. Below are examples of prompts for:

3.1. Defining the Problem: Prompt Example

  • How many people are “hungry”_ (insert your chosen problem area) in the world? What are the sources of the answer.
    If there are multiple answers choose the one(s) that have the most recent source publication dates and are the most relevant to your interests. If you don’t get that information you need, ask different questions, such as the following:
  • How many people are (for hunger-related problems) malnourished, starving, stunted, in danger of food shortages, etc. (plus sources for each question asked). [Insert alternative descriptors for your chosen problem area.]
  • Give me an overview of the world food (or other) problem based on latest UN reports.
  • What is the most serious problem related to world hunger (insert your chosen problem area)?
  • What are the major parts of the global _________ (insert your chosen problem area)?
  • Consider more complicated prompts— like: How many people are hungry in the world? What are the sources of the answer. Write for a well-informed audience. Use an academic tone. Make it concise. Summarize at end. 1
  • Remember that ChatGPT and Bard have a memory. They keeps track of what you wrote. You can use/build on this memory in your revisions to your prompts.

3.2. Present State

  • What are the major parts of the global _________ (insert your chosen problem area)?
  • What influences the state of the global ______ system?
  • What is the gap between the problem state and preferred state? For this prompt you will need to insert the present state from the “Defining the problem” prompt and your vision of the preferred state

3.3. Strategy

AI works well as a generator of ideas and options. It is good at volume. Not necessarily good ideas but lots of them—and buried within the many might be a good to very good idea, or one that will trigger a new idea in your mind, or some ideas that working together will be great combinations. The following are some prompts you with which you can start.

  • What are the alternatives for __________ (examples from food problem area: eliminating hunger in the world; increasing food production; increasing the amount of land being irrigated; reducing post-harvest food waste; making food affordable for everyone, etc. (Tip #1: Take one of the facets of your preferred state and insert it into the prompt. Tip #2: Tell the AI “You are an expert at strategic thinking, problem solving, design, and option generation. When asked to solve a problem you come up with creative, clever, interesting ideas. Your first task is to ________ (e.g eliminate hunger from the world). Describe the details of each way.
  • What is needed to increase food production in ____ (location where problem area is severest).
  • Who is needed to do this (the above)?
  • What resources are needed to make this happen? (to implement Increased food production)
  • What will it cost to do _________ How much money would it cost to reach the ideal state/eliminate hunger, feed everyone in the world?
  • Where could the money needed to do _______ come from?
  • What are possible outcomes of _______ (e.g. eliminating hunger, implementing x strategy or solution).

3.4. Implementation

  • What technology is needed to ____ (e.g eliminate hunger)?
  • What policies or programs are needed to ____ (e.g eliminate hunger)?
  • Which technology is the most efficient, effective, cost-effective, sustainable? What is a good test for _________ (one of the technologies from first prompt in this section)?Where is a good place to conduct this test?
  • What is a plan for scaling the _______ (technology from first prompt in this section)?

3.5. Initiative

  • Where do we go from here/next/what can I do with $100,000
  • Where could we get $100,000 to __________ (insert test of strategy)

3.6. Warnings

  • Sources for answers requiring the most up-to-date statistical data might not be available. It will depend on when the model was last updated. If this is the case, settle for what is available and then go to the latest statistical document. A good starting place is to check the Primer’s Appendix #2, List of Online Statistical Data Sources. These will take you to the latest available data.
  • The current state of LLMs is evolving rapidly. Whatever is written here needs to be viewed in this context— it could be/most likely will be at least a bit out-of-date by the time you are reading this. Nevertheless, this introductory overview should be helpful as a place to start.

Remember that each question/prompt for the LLM is an exploration. If you don’t get an answer that is helpful, ask the prompt in a different way. Invest some time in “prompt engineering”— editing and playing with prompts to come up with the results you want.
